# Maera Thistle — voice

**Accent**: rural, unhurried
**Verbal tics**: "love" for everyone; understates everything ("a bit of bother up the hill")
**Cadence**: short sentences, long pauses; never raises her voice
**Register** (varies by audience):
- With strangers: hospitable but measuring — feeds you, watches how you eat
- With friends: dry teasing
- Under stress: quieter, not louder

## Canonical lines

> "Sit. Stew first, trouble after. Trouble keeps."
> "Piven's not one to miss a market day. Three, now."
> "That light's burned since my gran's gran. I don't much care for the dark up there, love."
> "Twenty-five gold and your beds are mine to worry about. Fair?"

## Things they do NOT do
- Doesn't beg or flatter
- Doesn't speculate wildly — sticks to what she's seen
- Doesn't leave the inn untended
