# Setting overview

A deliberately small, system-neutral starting canvas: **the Vale**, a quiet
farming valley a week's ride from the nearest city, with the village of
**Emberwick** at its heart. Everything here is meant to be replaced or grown —
swap in the Sword Coast, your homebrew continent, or anything else during
session 0. Nothing is load-bearing.

## The region in one paragraph

The Vale is sheep country: a river, a mill, terraced pastures, and one road
that climbs out of the valley toward the wider world. Old shrines dot the
hills — leftovers of an earlier people nobody remembers clearly — and most
folk treat them with practical superstition: keep the lights lit, leave the
stones alone. Trouble here is small and personal, which makes it exactly the
right size for level 1.

## Major settlements

- **Emberwick** — the village. Population ~200. Inn, mill, smithy, market green. **This is where the party starts.**
- **Grayford** — a market town two days downriver. Referenced, not detailed.

## Tone targets

*(Set during session 0. Default: heroic fantasy with mortal stakes — the world
